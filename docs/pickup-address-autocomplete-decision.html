Website decision brief

Pickup address input — which option?

Choosing how customers enter a custom pickup address when they're not at one of your 77 listed hotels.

Prepared for Casa Venturas · 2026-04-19 · Project: micasaventuras.com rebuild · Requires ~5 min to decide

The problem

Today's El Yunque pickup field on the new website offers two paths:

  1. Listed hotel — customer searches from the 77 hotels you configured in Bókun (autocomplete works, 1-click selection, good UX).
  2. Custom address — customer clicks "Not staying at a listed hotel?" and types their address in a free text box.

The free text box is functional but has real problems:

Risk 1 — typos and incomplete addresses. "calle loiza" vs "Calle Loíza 1510" vs "1510 Loíza St.". Your driver on the day of the tour has to guess the right one.

Risk 2 — addresses outside your service area. Nothing stops a customer from typing a Dorado or Humacao address. You discover it the night before, and have to cancel.

Risk 3 — no GPS coordinates. Driver cannot tap the address in Waze or Google Maps — has to search manually each time, losing 2–3 minutes per pickup.

We want to add an address autocomplete — the same thing you see on Uber or Airbnb: as the customer types, real Puerto Rico addresses appear; they pick one; we store it accurately with GPS coordinates.

Why we recommend Google Places

★ Recommendation: Option C — Google Places Autocomplete

Three concrete reasons, in priority order:

  1. Your drivers already use Google Maps / Waze. When we save a Google place_id, the driver can tap the pickup from the Bókun dashboard and open Waze in one tap with exact coordinates. No guessing, no re-searching.
  2. Your customers recognize it instantly. "Type your address, pick from the suggestions" is the exact pattern Uber, Airbnb, DoorDash, and every major app uses. Zero learning curve.
  3. It costs you $0. At 40 bookings/day (well above today's volume) with ~5% custom addresses, you'd use ~0.2% of Google's free tier.

What you need to do if you say YES to Google Places

  1. Go to console.cloud.google.com (log in with any Google account — your personal Gmail works fine).
  2. Create a new project called "Casa Venturas Website".
  3. Enable the "Places API (New)" product — one click.
  4. Create an API key.
  5. Restrict the key (important — 2 minutes):
    • HTTP referrers: https://micasaventuras.com/* and http://localhost:3737/*
    • API restrictions: tick only "Places API (New)"
  6. Activate billing — Google requires a credit card on file even for free usage. You'll never be charged at your volume. Set a monthly budget alert at $5 for paranoia.
  7. Copy the key (looks like AIzaSyXXXXX...) and send it to us.

We plug it into the website, deploy, and the new autocomplete is live the same day.

The pros/cons of each option, side by side

A — Keep free text (do nothing)

Pros

  • Zero work, zero cost
  • Already shipped, proven to work

Cons

  • Typos waste driver time every morning
  • No GPS → hard to navigate
  • Customers can book from outside your service area

B — OpenStreetMap (free, open-source)

Pros

  • Zero cost, no billing setup
  • Open data, no vendor lock-in

Cons

  • Puerto Rico coverage thinner than Google — some small streets missing
  • Rate limit of 1 request/second (a problem during peak booking times)
  • Less polished autocomplete UX (slower, fewer suggestions)
  • No place_id for deep-link into Waze

C — Google Places (recommended)

Pros

  • Best Puerto Rico coverage (same data the USPS uses for shipping)
  • Instant, universally-recognized UX
  • place_id opens in Waze/Maps in one tap
  • $0/month at your volume, forever
  • Optional geo-restriction (country: PR) — blocks non-Puerto-Rico addresses

Cons

  • Requires a Google Cloud account + billing activation (~5 min)
  • Vendor dependency — if Google changes pricing, you'd have to migrate (unlikely for years)

D — Mapbox Search

Pros

  • Modern, clean UX — some designers prefer it to Google
  • Slightly larger free tier than Google

Cons

  • Less universally recognized than Google
  • Puerto Rico coverage slightly weaker than Google
  • Same setup work as Google (account + billing)
